Come explore California's beautiful wilderness


Always open. Reservations are not avalible or required to enter the park.

Not just a great valley, but a shrine to human foresight, the strength of franite, the power of glaciers, the persistence of life, and the tranquility of the Hish Sierra. First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more.

Fire is a natural and essential part if Yosemite. We manage fire carefully and study how it interacts with the park's ecosystems. This blog provides updates abiut fires in Yosemite. Check for current fire restrictions.

We strongly recommend that you make reservations for lodging, camping, and backpacking. You can pay the park entrance fee upon arrival (there is no need to pay it in advance).
